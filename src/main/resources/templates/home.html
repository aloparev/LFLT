Stock-Picking Experiment

Inspired by Daniel Kahneman's "Thinking, Fast and Slow"

The Illusion of Validity

In chapter 20, "The Illusion of Validity", Kahneman argues that there is no such thing as a stock-picking skill, but only an illusion of it. He illustrates that even the professional stock pickers (on average) fail the basic test of skill - permanent achievement. I.e. the traders are playing the game of chance, because there is no correlation between their picks and their profits.

What is it about?

So, if even the professional portfolio managers are rolling the dice, why not try it as well? Let's take the available stocks, randomly buy some of them and monitor the performance. That's exactly what this project, "Low Frequency Luck Trading", is doing. I.e. LFLT performs blind guesses about what stock to buy and then monitors the portfolio performance over time.
