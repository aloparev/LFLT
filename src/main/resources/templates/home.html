Low-Frequency Luck Trading

Inspired by Daniel Kahneman's "Thinking, Fast and Slow"

The Illusion of Validity

In chapter 20, "The Illusion of Validity", Kahneman argues that so called stock-picking skill is somewhat an illusion. He illustrates that even the professional stock traders (on average) fail the basic test of skill - permanent achievement. In other words - the traders play the game of chance since there is no correlation between their picks and their profits.

What is it about?

Now, what we are doing here is testing the premise made earlier. Under the experiment, we randomly buy some of the S&P 500 stocks to see how they will perform over time. If you register, there is an option to create your own portfolio and manage it with nominal dollars to try and beat the market. Furthermore, you can compete against the random buy.
