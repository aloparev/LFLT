Stock-Picking Experiment

Inspired by Daniel Kahneman's "Thinking, Fast and Slow".

The Illusion of Validity

In chapter 20, called "The Illusion of Validity", Kahneman argues that there is no such thing as a stock-picking skill, but only an illusion of it. He illustrates that even the professional stock pickers (on average) fail the basic test of skill - permanent achievement. I.e. the traders are playing the game of chance, because they are making educated guesses in a situation of great uncertainty.

LFLT

LFLT is an acronym for "Low Frequency Luck Trading". The aim of LFLT is to demonstrate how stock picking is done by blind guesses and how a stock portfolio with such picks performs over time.
